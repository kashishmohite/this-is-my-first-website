Sham Valley

♦About Sham Valley:

Sham Valley, nicknamed the "Apricot Valley", is a Himalayan valley along the Indus River and NH1 - Srinagar-Kargil-Leh National Highway in the Sham district of the Indian Union Territory of Ladakh. The valley is located around 170 kilometres from Kargil and 180 kilometres from the Ladakh's capital Leh. The nearest airport is Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport in Leh. The valley, which starts from Spituk and also includes area till Lamayouro, is well known amongst trekkers.The valley is 32 km long and it takes 4-5 days to complete the trek. The maximum elevation is 3874 metres.Likir village located in the valley is famous for its Buddhist Monastery.
